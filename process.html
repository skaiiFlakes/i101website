  • Week 0

    Individual Research

    Our team began this project by conducting individual research on underrepresented innovators in the fields of technology, computer science, and informatics. Each team member researched 2 innovators and recorded how the innovator was underrepresented, what challenges they faced and how they overcame them, and what contributions they made in their respective field.

  • Week 1

    Cynthia Breazeal

    Our team came together and discussed who we wanted to highlight for this project. We decided on Cynthia Breazeal because we think that her research in the field of social robotics will be extremely important in the coming years. Additionally, we found it inspiring that her experience having children helps motivate her work, as children are often thought of as a burden on working women. We also created a project charter, which outlined our innovator choice, project timeline, and backup plans in the event of exposures.

  • Week 2

    Group Research

    During this week, our team gathered more in depth information about Breazeal as well as media assets. We learned about her general life, how she is underrepresented in the field of technology, her contributions to robotics, and the future promise of her research. This information is covered on the About and Innovations pages.

  • Week 3

    Wireframing

    At this point in the project we created the website framework for how we wanted the website to look. We took other websites into consideration for what style we wanted to convey. In the end, we chose a modern style. Our team searched the internet for visuals that would enhance the websites feel without overcrowding it and losing focus. We made sure to include information about Breazeal as well as information on our team and this process page.

  • Week 4

    Website Drafting

    Working off of the wireframe, it was time to create the real website. Having a structured blueprint to work off of was immensely helpful in our website coming to life. Similar to the wireframe, separate pages were created to give our website depth. With our draft, there were still some sections left uncompleted, which we worked on the following week.

  • Week 5

    Website Feedback

    This week, we took our website and tested it with real users. Each member of our team interviewed someone and gave them full access to the website. We had assigned tasks we needed them to do to test our website's navigation. Overall, the users found our site easy to navigate. We got some great critiques on how to further improve the website from an outside perspective that none of us thought of.

  • Week 6

    Presentation & Final Submission

    After hearing feedback, we took the suggestions and improved our website. This week we worked on those suggestions as well as the presentation. The presentation was a large overview of Cynthia Breazeal and our work on the website.

Instrument and Summary of Feedback

We found that several of our test users had issues navigating the website due to non-functioning buttons. In response to this, we made sure to streamline the final website to be as simple and user friendly as possible. This also compliments the minimalist aesthetic of our website, which test users generally liked. Other feedback was mostly minor errors such as spelling errors and removing filler text. This feedback was also valuable, as small mistakes like this are typically easier to find for people not already familiar with the website.

Reflection

Our group worked greatly together by splitting up our work and keeping each member accountable. Every milestone, we had set tasks of what we needed to do and who was submitting. If someone needed a reminder, any of us could kindly text in our group chat and we would respond quickly. For tips for future groups, we could all recommend taking criticism and using it to improve and not take it personally. If we had to do it all over again, we agreed we could have started assignments earlier to not feel rushed at the end of the week. For the most part, we would be done early but a couple milestones required more effort. Our group had a relaxed atmosphere while still able to get things done, and that helped tremendously. All of us never felt worried about asking for help from one another.
